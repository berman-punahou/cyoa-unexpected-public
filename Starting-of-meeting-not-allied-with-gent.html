An imposing man comes to stand in the middle of the concentric circle of tables. After clearing his throat a few times, he introduces himself as the emergency president of the conference. You remember voting for him, you saw him as an unbiased mediator. "Today we are gathered here to solve a great issue. As we all know, the world is cooling down at rates faster than we could have imagined. We must band together and find a way to end this cold." He asks you and the other delegates to vote on the three major issues facing the world: The new type of quickly spreading cancer that is obliterating entire cities, the food crisis, and of course, the unending cold.
